The Model

Bistable gene expression systems are, in a sense, the cellular equivalent of a transistor and therefore may function within the cell as a component in performing logical operations. In practice some switching mechanisms can be controlled by varying some continuous (experimentally controllable) parameters. However, when attempting to model bistable systems, the parameter estimates obtained via the standard fitting procedure are often of low accuracy due to the variability of biological experiments. This effect is pronounced in unoptimized experiments. Optimal experimental design (OED) procedures allow experimenters to select the experiment which best reduces parameter estimation error.
